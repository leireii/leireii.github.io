Writing a Summary

face
There are individual exercises in this section.

In writing a summary, the writer takes the "main meat" of the original piece, leaving out any descriptive details, examples, or direct quotes. Writers of summaries never include their own personal opinions, responses, or recommendations concerning the subject of the summary. Summary writing is like the ideal of journalistic reporting, which aspires to present only the main facts and issues in concise form.
