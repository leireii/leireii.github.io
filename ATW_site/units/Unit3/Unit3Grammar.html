Past Perfect

face
There are individual exercises in this section.

The past perfect tense can be used to describe an action that began in the past that preceded another past action. The past perfect is formed by using the past form of the verb have, that is, had, with the past participle of the main verb:


Subject Past form of to have Past participle Complement Time (less distant in the past)
I had heard about war-torn areas before I came to Dili.
