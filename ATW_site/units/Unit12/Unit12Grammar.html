assignment

The Gerund

In certain lines of the reading passage, What are the UN's Nobel Prizes?, notice how verbs that follow prepositions always take a gerund (verb + ing) form:

"In awarding the Nobel Prize..."
"... its efforts in the area of promoting human rights, of fighting HIV/AIDS..."
"...UN Peacekeeping Forces were awarded the Prize for reducing tensions and for making a decisive contribution toward initiating peace negotiations..."

Notice how the gerund follows the prepositions in these common expressions:

be interested in + gerund (example: He is interested in learning more about the history of the Nobel Prize.) dream about + gerund (example: Many in the world dream about having true international cooperation.)
