Future-in-the-Past Form

face
There are individual exercises in this section.

The future-in-the-past can be a difficult structure to grasp. It is used to express an action or state to take place in the futureâ€”but from the point-of-view of a time in the past. The structure is formed in two ways.

One way is to combine the simple past tense form of the verb be and the infinitive form of the main or principle verb.


Subject Past form of to be Infinitive of main verb Rest of sentence
Each member state was to have a vote in the General Assembly.

Note: This form cannot be used if preceded by the verb, "hope."

face

Exercise


The founding member-states decided that the Secretary-General (serve) a five-year term.

The second way to form the future-in-the-past is by combining the modal verb would (the past tense of the future of will) with the base form of the main verb.


Subject Modal verb would Base form of main verb Rest of sentence
The Charter would oblige member-states to act on its decisions.

Note: this form can be used if preceded by the verb, "hope."

Exercise


The founders hoped that member-states (abide) by the Charter.
